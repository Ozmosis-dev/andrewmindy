03 — About

Designer Turned
Systems Builder

I started as a designer, spent four years on a sales floor closing deals and building training systems, and evolved into someone who can own the full stack — from brand strategy to custom automation.

That sales floor experience changed how I think about creative work. When you've sold $160K+ per month and watched reps succeed or struggle based on whether the system supported them, you stop designing for awards. You design for conversion. You build systems that remove friction and create predictability.

Now I work with companies that have outgrown their current tools and processes. The brand that got you to $1M won't get you to $10M. The manual workflows that worked with five people break down with fifty. I help systematize what's working so it scales without you.

04 — How I Work

Three Situations
I Solve

I work with companies facing one or more of these situations:

01

Outgrowing Current Systems

Your team is spending hours on manual tasks. Lead follow-up is inconsistent. Data lives in spreadsheets instead of dashboards.

Audit your workflows, identify bottlenecks, and build automation that reclaims time and eliminates friction.

02

Brand Doesn't Match Who You've Become

Your website was built three years ago and feels dated. Prospects question whether you can handle their scale.

Rebuild from strategy through launch. Brand positioning, identity systems, and conversion-focused execution.

03

Need to Move Fast Without Hiring Three People

You need brand work, a new website, marketing automation, and operational improvements. Hiring specialists for each is slow.

Take ownership end-to-end. One engagement, from discovery through deployment.

The Process

Every Engagement
Follows This Framework

01

Discovery

Understand the real problem, not just the symptoms. Map current workflows, identify friction points, and align on success metrics.

02

Strategy

Define the approach. What gets built, in what order, and how we'll measure success. Clear scope, realistic timeline, no surprises.

03

Build

Execute with regular check-ins. You see progress as it happens, not all at once at the end.

04

Optimize

Measure results, refine based on data, and hand off or continue based on what you need.
